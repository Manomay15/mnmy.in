Circa 2017 - I came home after school, picked up the Times of India and flipped straight to the last pages to read what Sunil Gavaskar had to say on a random Tuesday evening. I felt it gave me an intellectual edge in any cricket conversation. These 2 pages were filled with match reviews, stats, interviews and also a small column on today’s schedule and where to watch the game. That was probably the most dopamine inducing thing I read every day, even after 6 hours of school. The last pages were always sports, I never bothered with what was written before or anything not cricket. Too rigid in my ways as a 14 year old.

Once on a hot summer evening with no electricity, tv, a phone or internet & after reading every word from the last 2 pages, twice, I had no choice but to indulge myself in foreign subjects like politics, economics, corruption and bollywood. The 4th pillar of our democracy. Yesterday Instagram decided to randomly throw a reel of adults slapping cheese slices on babies to stop them from crying. I never knew it existed. I never knew I’d like it. Felt the same when I discovered the world of politics, economics, corruption and surprisingly bollywood. I never knew it existed. I never knew I’d like it. Read through all 30 pages that day & even more from last week, the same way I doomscrolled baby cheese slice slapping videos till 3 AM yesterday.

Let’s talk about the road you’ll never take, the book you’ll never read, the bird you’ll never see, and the band that you’ll never listen to. The algorithm has told you a lot many things that you either didn’t want to find out or are just not what you need. I have no idea why “the algorithm” sounds like “The Algorithm” - a proper noun. It is not any algorithm, it is “The Algorithm”.

You just know a lot more things now and sadly, everyone tells you what you have to experience or feel after going to that underrated picturesque place, reading that traumatising book or listening to heavy metal. You’ll skip that because you know what it will feel like & maybe you are not in the mood for that feeling. Big mistake. Not everyone feels the same way about anything. This is the same reason I feel movie reviews are a scam. Don’t tell me what I have to feel after watching a movie please! I can’t expect everyone to have the same love for adults slapping babies with cheese slices like I have. It is very difficult to control the orange part of the venn diagram but the blue (read: cyan) is very much in our hands.
